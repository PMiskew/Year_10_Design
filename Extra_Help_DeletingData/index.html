    • Passing

      Passing Drills

      Monkey in the Middle


      Two players are on offense. One player in the middle is the monkey and is on defense. Offensive players pass the ball back and forth. They use fakes to get the ball past the defender. No lob passes are allowed.

      Wait for the defender to recover to the offense before throwing the next pass. When the defender tips a pass, the passer goes to be the monkey in the middle.
      Wall Passing

      Wall passing is a great way to improve hand-eye coordination and athletic strength while developing passing, catching, and ball handling skills.

      In the video, there are 3 progressions:

      • Progression 1: Overhead Wall Pass - 25 Reps

      • Progression 2: 1 Handling Alternating Wall Pass - 20 Reps

      • Progression 3: 1 Hand Wall Pass - 20 Reps With Each Hand
    • Dribbling

      Dribbling Drills

      Two Up, Two Back
      Step 1: Set a chair or cone about 21 feet from the basket, and take 2 hard dribbles at the chair.
      Step 2: When reaching the chair, take 2 backup dribbles.
      Step 3: Use a change of direction dribble (cross over, inside out, etc). Then push ahead into a 1 dribble lay-up or pull-up jumpshot.
      This drill practices ballhandling in regard to change of direction. It can be done individually or with teammates.

      Dribble Screens
      Step 1: Set a chair 20 feet out at the foul line extended. Start at mid-court with the ball.
      Step 2: Dribble and take the ball below the chair.
      Step 3: Once below the chair, make a change dribble and come off it, simulating a ball screen. You can now finish with a shot or take it to the rim.
      This drill practices coming off ball screens from the wing. It can be done individually or with teammates.
    • Shooting

      Shooting Drills

      Form Shooting


      This drill reinforces good technique of the "release" portion of one's shot. Feet shoulder width apart, make an L with your shooting arm and push the ball up and out with one hand.

      Focus on pushing the ball through the fingers. The index or middle finger should be pointing at the hoop with the follow through. The video to the left demonstrates several reps of the drill.
      Chair Curl
      Setup: Place a chair, foul line extended, and put a ball on the chair. Start at the side block on the same side as the chair.

      Step 1: Sprint and curl around the chair (it can be from the baseline or over the top), pick up the ball and take a jump shot.
      Step 2: After taking the jump shot, chase down the rebound (put it in if the jumper was missed - always end on a make).
      Step 3: make 1 or 2 change dribble moves back around the chair and take a 1 dribble jump shot.
      This drill simulates coming off of a screen for a shot. It combines shooting, ballhandling and high intensity change in direction.
